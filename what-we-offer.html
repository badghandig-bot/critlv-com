Coverage / 03

What “coverage” actually means.

Three intersecting layers. Designed together, installed together, documented together.

Connectivity

Every workstation, conference room, camera and AP gets a certified Cat6/6A drop back to MDF or IDF. Fluke tested, labeled both ends.

CAT6/6A OM4 FIBER <99% PASS

Camera Coverage

Every entrance, exit, corridor intersection and high-value zone is covered. Overlapping fields of view, zero exterior blind spots.

IP / 4K 30D RETENTION OVERLAP 15%

Wi-Fi Coverage

RF surveys before AP placement. Roaming hand-offs, dedicated guest VLAN, and capacity for the actual user count — not just the floorplan.

802.11AX −65 dBm FLOOR CHANNEL PLAN
Timeline / 04

Six weeks, end‑to‑end.

A typical Standard tier install. Larger jobs scale linearly — same milestones, longer pulls.

WK 01
Site walk + design

Walk every closet, ceiling and pathway. Drawings, BOM and labeling scheme delivered end-of-week.

WK 02
Mobilization

Material on site, J-hooks set, fire-stop sleeves placed, MDF/IDF racks staged.

WK 03–04
Cable pull

Trunks, drops and fiber backbone. Photos uploaded daily to your shared project folder.

WK 05
Termination + test

Patch panels, jacks, every run Fluke certified. Rework on the spot for any failed pair.

WK 06
Devices + commission

Cameras, APs, readers and panels installed and configured. Integrated with your VMS, IDP and IT directory.

WK 07
Walkthrough + handoff

On-site walkthrough with your team. As-builts, test reports, credentials and documentation binder delivered.

Handoff / 05

Fluke test reports
