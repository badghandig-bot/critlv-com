Who we are / 01

A small Triangle outfit that promotes from within.

CRIT is owned and operated by Ryan Cooke and Juan Clark. We are a Raleigh low-voltage shop, and we believe the only way to grow the business is to grow the people in it.

That means we hire for attitude and trainability, not paperwork. You do not need a certificate, a degree, or prior trade experience to walk in. You need to want to learn, be willing to show up on time, and be okay getting your hands dirty in a ceiling space. We will teach you the rest.

The reason we run hiring this way: every tech we bring on is someone we are personally invested in. Ryan or Juan trains you. Ryan or Juan answers your questions on the job. There is no HR layer between you and the owners — if you have a problem, you walk into the office and tell us. We will fix it.

The pay / 02

What you'll learn / 03

Real work, real skills.

You learn on actual customer jobs, not in a classroom. Here is what the work covers across a typical first year.

Structured Cabling

Pulling and terminating Cat5e, Cat6 and Cat6A. Patch panels, jacks, labeling, cable management.

Smart-Home & IoT

Installing connected devices, hubs, controllers. Wiring backbones for cameras, audio, and IoT gear in residential builds.

IP Cameras & NVR

Mounting cameras, running PoE drops, setting up NVR recorders with motion zones, alerts, and remote viewing.

New-Construction Low Voltage

Pre-wire work before the drywall goes up. Reading plans, working alongside other trades, coordinating with GCs.

On-Site Troubleshooting

Tracing existing cable plants, diagnosing what is broken, fixing it without breaking what works.

Customer Communication

Showing up clean, explaining the work in plain English, leaving a job site better than you found it. This is half the job.

Who fits / 04

What we look for.

  • You show up on time. Reliability is the foundation; everything else can be taught.
  • You have a valid driver’s license and reliable transportation. Field work means getting to job sites across the Triangle.
  • You are willing to learn. We assume you do not know low-voltage work yet — that is fine. We assume you want to.
  • You are good with your hands. You can use basic tools and you are not afraid of ladders, attics, or crawl spaces.
  • You can talk to customers. Most of our jobs are in someone’s home or business. You represent CRIT every time you walk through a door.
  • You are okay being trusted. As you grow we hand you more responsibility — running jobs, leading other techs, talking directly with customers about scope.
